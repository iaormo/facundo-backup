## DLP Insurance

**Industry:** Insurance (likely life, health, or general coverage)
**Primary Challenge:** Trust building + education (insurance is complex and people avoid thinking about it)
**Target Audiences:**
- Primary: 30-55 year olds, families, professionals
- Secondary: Business owners (if commercial lines)

### Platform Strategy
**LinkedIn** (40% of effort)
- Thought leadership on risk management
- "Insurance myths busted" content series
- Agent/producer spotlights (humanize the brand)
- Company culture for recruiting

**Facebook** (35% of effort)  
- Community engagement
- Educational carousels (simple explanations)
- Customer testimonials
- Live Q&A sessions on common questions

**Instagram** (15% of effort)
- Behind the scenes
- Team highlights
- Customer success stories (with permission)
- Stories for quick tips

**YouTube** (10% of effort)
- Long-form educational content
- "Insurance explained" series
- Agent interview series

### Content Pillars
1. **Education (40%)** - "What is [insurance term] and why you need it"
2. **Trust/Social Proof (25%)** - Claims stories, testimonials, years in business
3. **Community (20%)** - Local events, sponsorships, team culture
4. **Promotional (15%)** - Open enrollment reminders, policy reviews, referrals

### Ad Strategy
**Meta Ads:**
- Lead magnets: "Free Insurance Checklist", "5 Mistakes That Cost You Coverage"
- Retargeting: Website visitors → quote request
- Lookalike: Based on current customer list
- Video testimonials for awareness

**Google Ads:**
- High-intent keywords: "insurance quote [city]", "best [type] insurance"
- Local service ads if applicable
- YouTube pre-roll for brand awareness

**Key Metrics:**
- Cost per lead (target: $15-40 depending on policy type)
- Quote request conversion rate
- Policy bound rate from leads

### Unique Angles
- "Real claims that saved families" stories
- Insurance myth-busting (common misconceptions)
- Agent personality content (people buy from people)
- Annual review reminders (existing customers)

## BotBuilders

**Industry:** Chatbot development / AI automation agency
**Primary Challenge:** Education (people don't know what's possible) + standing out in crowded AI space
**Target Audiences:**
- Primary: Small business owners, e-commerce operators
- Secondary: Marketing agencies, enterprise ops teams

### Platform Strategy
**LinkedIn** (50% of effort)
- Primary B2B channel
- Automation case studies
- "Before/After" workflow breakdowns
- ROI calculators and proof

**YouTube** (25% of effort)
- Tutorial content (how to build bots)
- Platform comparisons
- Client success stories
- Screen recordings of bot flows

**Twitter/X** (15% of effort)
- Quick tips and insights
- Industry commentary
- Engage with automation/builder community
- Thread content (educational)

**Instagram** (10% of effort)
- Behind the scenes of builds
- Team/process content
- Quick tips in Reels
- Client results screenshots

### Content Pillars
1. **Education (35%)** - "How to automate [specific task]"
2. **Results/Proof (30%)** - Case studies, metrics, ROI
3. **Process (20%)** - How bots are built, behind the scenes
4. **Industry/Trends (15%)** - AI news, platform updates

### Ad Strategy
**Meta Ads:**
- Lead magnet: "Automation Audit Checklist" or "10 Tasks You Should Automate"
- Webinar funnel: "How to Build Your First Bot"
- Case study ads: "How [Client] Saved 20 Hours/Week"
- Retargeting: Website visitors → free consultation

**LinkedIn Ads:**
- Sponsored content for case studies
- Message ads for direct outreach
- Lead gen forms for whitepapers

**Google Ads:**
- "Chatbot builder"
- "WhatsApp automation"
- "AI customer service"
- Competitor keywords

**Key Metrics:**
- Cost per consultation booked ($50-150 target)
- Demo-to-close rate
- Customer acquisition cost vs. lifetime value
- Content engagement (shares = awareness)

### Unique Angles
- "Bot vs Human" comparison humor
- Screen recordings of impressive automations
- "Day in the life" of a conversation designer
- ROI calculators and savings estimates
- Platform-specific content (WhatsApp vs. Messenger vs. website)

### Partnership Opportunities
- Web agencies (white-label)
- Marketing consultants (referral)
- SaaS companies (integrations)
- Business coaches (audience overlap)

## CROSS-CLIENT UNIVERSALS

### Weekly Rhythm (Per Client)
**Monday:** Plan week, schedule posts, check metrics
**Tuesday-Wednesday:** Content creation batch
**Thursday:** Community engagement, respond to comments/DMs
**Friday:** Analytics review, plan next week

### Monthly Rhythm
- Performance report (reach, engagement, leads, conversions)
- Content audit (what worked, what didn't)
- Creative refresh (new hooks, new angles)
- Competitor check (what are they doing?)

### Quarterly Rhythm  
- Strategy review (platforms still right?)
- Audience research refresh
- Major campaign planning
- Budget reallocation based on performance

### Content Templates (Universal)
1. **The List Post** - "5 ways to [result]"
2. **The Myth Buster** - "Everyone thinks X, but actually Y"
3. **The Case Study** - "How [client] achieved [result]"
4. **The Process Peek** - Behind the scenes
5. **The Quick Win** - Immediate actionable tip
6. **The Story** - Personal/client journey
7. **The Comparison** - "This vs That"
8. **The Hot Take** - Contrarian opinion (careful)
