## Phase 1: Legal Foundation (Weeks 1–2)

### Week 1: DTI Permit
- [ ] Register business name with DTI (Department of Trade and Industry)
- [ ] Choose structure: sole proprietorship (fastest, simplest)
- [ ] Online registration at bnrs.dti.gov.ph
- [ ] Documents needed: valid ID, email, mobile number
- [ ] Cost: ~200 PHP
- [ ] **Output:** DTI certificate of business registration

### Week 2: Business Registration
- [ ] Barangay clearance (local barangay office)
- [ ] Mayor's permit (city/municipality hall)
- [ ] BIR registration (tax identification)
- [ ] Open business bank account
- [ ] Get business insurance (vehicle, liability)

**Checkpoint:** All legal docs ready before any public launch.

## Critical Success Factors

### 1. Vehicle Maintenance Fund
Set aside 20% of every rental for maintenance. Jimny repairs are expensive. Plan for tires, oil changes, unexpected issues.

### 2. Calendar Discipline
Block maintenance days. Do not overbook. One breakdown hurts reputation more than one lost booking.

### 3. Insurance
Get comprehensive vehicle insurance that covers rentals. Get liability insurance. One accident without coverage can end the business.

### 4. Boundaries
- No off-roading without orientation
- No smoking in vehicle (5,000 PHP cleaning fee)
- Return with same fuel level or refuel charge applies
- Late returns: 500 PHP/hour
