### 3. TOP-PERFORMING EMAIL & LINKEDIN STRATEGIES (2026)

**Email Best Practices:**
- **Length:** 80-120 words for highest response rates (technical audiences tolerate 150-180 words)
- **Send timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am recipient's local time zone
- **Subject lines:** 6-8 words, question format or urgency (without ALL CAPS)
- **Personalization:** Reference specific device type, recent funding, or regulatory milestone
- **Single CTA:** Don't give multiple options; one clear next step
- **Follow-up cadence:** Day 5, Day 10, Day 20 (final)

**LinkedIn Best Practices:**
- **Connection requests:** Custom notes have 45% acceptance vs. 30% without
- **Follow-up timing:** 2-3 days after acceptance (not immediately)
- **Message length:** Under 400 characters when possible (22% response boost)
- **Engagement strategy:** Comment on posts, reference recent activity (32% lift)
- **Value-first approach:** Share insights before asking for meetings

**Cold Outreach Conversion Funnel:**
- Open rate: 20-25% (industry average)
- Response rate: 2-5% (cold), 8-12% (warm)
- Meeting booking rate: 40-50% of responses
- Overall conversion: 1-2% of cold emails → meetings

## QMSR CHANGES & IMPLICATIONS

### What Changed on February 2, 2026

The **Quality Management System Regulation (QMSR)** officially replaced the **Quality System Regulation (QSR)** on February 2, 2026, marking the biggest shift in device quality requirements in 27 years.

**Key Changes:**
1. **ISO 13485:2016 Harmonization:** FDA aligned with international standard terminology and requirements
2. **Management Review Inspections:** FDA can now inspect management reviews; must demonstrate strategic value, not checkbox compliance
3. **Device Master Record (DMR) → Medical Device File (MDF):** Documentation architecture updated to ISO terminology
4. **Risk Management Integration:** ISO 14971 risk management more tightly integrated with QMS
5. **Supplier Controls:** Enhanced expectations for supplier qualification and monitoring

### Impact on Avania Campaigns

**IMMEDIATE OPPORTUNITIES (Hot Market):**
1. **QMSR Readiness Assessments:** Companies need urgent gap analysis and action plans
2. **Management Review Restructuring:** New FDA inspection focus creates compliance urgency
3. **DMR Transition Support:** Documentation architecture updates needed across all device manufacturers
4. **Inspection Readiness:** First post-QMSR FDA inspections happening now; findings will set precedent

**POSITIONING SHIFTS:**
- **OLD (Dec 2025):** "Prepare for QMSR before it takes effect"
- **NEW (Feb 2026):** "QMSR is live—what needs to change in your QMS immediately?"

**Campaign Integration:**
- **Angela Johnson:** QMSR positioning as strategic regulatory transition, not just compliance burden
- **Catriona Boyd:** PRIMARY QMSR champion; quality systems compliance and inspection readiness
- **Sam Engelman:** QMSR's impact on software device QMS and validation documentation
- **Jasmine Saba:** QMSR as investor due diligence consideration
- **Jeannine Umpleby:** QMSR as timely hook for new client acquisition
